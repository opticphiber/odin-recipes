Kyercheh

Ingredients

  • Milk
  • Water
  • Kefir
  • Yeast
  • Sugar

Preparation

Kymis is a fermented, fizzy, foaming drink, which is most often made from cow's milk. This drink is slightly intoxicating, as during its preparation, fermentation processes occur in the milk due to bacteria and yeast. The nice thing is that it is very healthy and this drink can be made at home!

To prepare this drink, it is better to use not just milk, but steamed cow's milk. Tatars, Kazakhs and Mongols prepare the national drink in wooden or clay barrels, but if you do not have such containers at hand, a glass jar will serve as an excellent substitute. So, pour into it milk and boiled water, being sure to adhere to the proportions (1 cup of water to 3 cups of milk). To the same add kefir or plain sourdough, honey or sugar and mix everything well with a tablespoon until the last component is completely dissolved. As for the sweet component of the dish! It is preferable to add to Koumiss, of course, honey, as the drink becomes more flavorful and even more useful. So, cover the jar with gauze, so that our mixture did not get other objects, such as gnats, and put in a warm place for 12-16 hours. When you see a white concentrated sediment at the bottom, the first stage of fermentation is complete!
